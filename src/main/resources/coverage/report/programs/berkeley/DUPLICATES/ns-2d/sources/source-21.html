21 /*
22  * Given a locker T and a record R, a WriteLockInfo stores the info needed to
23  * undo the write ops (insertions, deletions, or updates) performed by T on
24  * R, if T aborts. Specifically, it stores the info needed to restore R to its
25  * "abort" version, i.e., the version of R that existed at the time when T
26  * locked R for the 1st time (and before T actually performed its 1st write
27  * op on R). Given that only transactional lockers may abort, WriteLockInfo
28  * are used by Txn lockers only.
29  *
30  * Notice that, conceptually, to write R, T locks R in exclusive mode and
31  * retains that lock until it terminates (commits or aborts). In other words,
32  * T locks R only once. However, because locks are acquired on LSNs and LSNs
33  * change every time a record is written, T may actually lock R multiple times,
34  * Everytime T locks a new LSN of R, it creates a new WriteLockIno. However,
35  * care is taken so that all WriteLockInfos for the same logical record and
36  * the same txn store the same info about the abort version (for details see
37  * CursorImpl.LockStanding, CursorImpl.lockLN(), and LN.logInternals()). In
38  * fact, if T writes R multiple times and then aborts, only one of the logrecs
39  * generated by T for R will be undone, restoring R to its abort version; the
40  * rest will be skipped. 
41  *
42  * Info about the aort version is needed during commit as well. Specifically,
43  * its LSN and on-disk size are needed to count the abort version obsolete
44  * when T commits.
45  */
