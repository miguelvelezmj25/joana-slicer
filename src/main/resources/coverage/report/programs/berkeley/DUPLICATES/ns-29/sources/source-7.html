26 /**
27  * The VLSNRecoveryTracker is used as a transient tracker at recovery time.
28  * It gathers up VLSN->LSN mappings that are in the log, but not persisted to
29  * the mapping database. It has somewhat different preconditions from the
30  * parent VLSNTracker which affect the semantics of tracking.
31  *
32  * Unlike VLSNTracker, the track() method is guaranteed to be executed in a
33  * serial fashion. In addition, this tracker needs to "discover" where the
34  * range for this tracker should start.  For example, suppose the on-disk
35  * VLSNIndex covers VLSNs 25 -> 200. Also, suppose that the recovery portion of
36  * the log holds VLSNs 190 -> 210 (an overlap of 190 -> 200)
37  *
38  * The VLSNIndex will be initialized with a range of 25 -> 200. We want the
39  * recovery tracker to hold VLSN mappings from 190 -> 210. We don't want it to
40  * just consult its range to determine where the next bucket starts.  If we did
41  * that, the recovery tracker would start at VLSN 1.
42  *
43  * The VLSNRecoveryTracker must account for rollbacks and invisible log
44  * entries. It has the authoritative view of what is in the recovery part of the
45  * log and will override what is in the on-disk tracker.  At merge time, the
46  * regular VLSNIndex must consult the VLSNRecoveryTracker's notion of what the
47  * highest VLSN value is.
48  *
49  * If we see a RollbackStart, the end of range is abruptly reset back to the
50  * matchpoint start. If we see non-invisible entries, the end of range may be
51  * incrementing. For example, suppose the log has:recovery tracker
52  *
53  * VLSN 10                                tracks 10
54  * VLSN 11 (invisible)                    skips
55  * VLSN 12 (invisible)                    skips
56  * VLSN 13 (invisible)                    skips
57  * rollback start to VLSN 9               truncates to 9, clear everything
58  * VLSN 10                                tracks 10
59  * VLSN 11                                tracks 11
60  * VLSN 12                                tracks 12
61  * rollback start to VLSN 11              truncates to 11
62  *
63  * Suppose the on-disk VLSNIndex holds mappings for VLSN 1->13. A merge of the
64  * VLSN index and the recovery tracker would
65  *   1) truncate any VLSN > than the recovery tracker's high point -- so the
66  *      VLSN index will drop mappings 12, 13
67  *   2) will replace any VLSN index mappings with those held in the recovery
68  *      tracker.
69  * The VLSNIndex should map 1 -> 11, with the 10 and 11 mapping provided by the
70  * recovery tracker.
71  */
