21 /**
22  * <p>
23  * This exception is thrown at the time of a commit in a Master, if the Master
24  * could not obtain transaction commit acknowledgments from its Replicas in
25  * accordance with the {@link ReplicaAckPolicy} currently in effect and within
26  * the requested timeout interval. This exception will never be thrown when the
27  * {@code ReplicaAckPolicy} of {@link ReplicaAckPolicy#NONE NONE} is in effect.
28  * <p>
29  * Note that an {@link InsufficientAcksException} means the transaction has
30  * already committed at the master. The transaction may also have been
31  * committed at one or more Replicas, but the lack of replica acknowledgments
32  * means that the number of replicas that committed could not be
33  * established. If the transaction was in fact committed by less than a simple
34  * majority of the nodes, it could result in a {@link RollbackException} when
35  * the node subsequently attempts to rejoin the group as a Replica.
36  * <p>
37  * The application can handle the exception and choose to respond in a number
38  * of ways. For example, it can
39  * <ul>
40  * <li>do nothing, assuming that the transaction will eventually propagate to
41  * enough replicas to become durable,
42  * <li>retry the operation in a new transaction, which may succeed or fail
43  * depending on whether the underlying problems have been resolved,
44  * <li>retry using a larger timeout interval and return to the original
45  * timeout interval at a later time,
46  * <li>fall back temporarily to a read-only mode,
47  * <li>increase the durability of the transaction on the Master by ensuring
48  * that the changes are flushed to the operating system's buffers or to
49  * the disk, or
50  * <li>give up and report an error at a higher level, perhaps to allow an
51  * administrator to check the underlying cause of the failure.
52  * </ul>
53  *
54  * @see Durability
55  */
